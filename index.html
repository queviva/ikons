that scene is composed of a series of ikons - svg images that can be instanciated and styled with css, or simply used as the src in an img tag

take a look at Rex - here he is as just an image tag, in his default state: a little bit of shade, a very faint logo on his prescription sweater, a white hat; he's just an image, nothing happens if you mouse-over him, or anything
now let's look at people like the objects they are! here's Poppy as the data attribute of an object tag - now if you mouse-over him, you'll see a list of css-variables that can be used to customize him when he's finally instanciated in his most useful carnation
finally we have Rex here as an svg use statement that has been customized for our own pleasure; when used this way, it's possible to have ikons dynamically match the page's themes

···
the actual best way to include svg is: as a use statement ikon; this combines the "ease of maintenance" of an external file with the css maniplability of inline svg 

            
<svg viewBox="0 0 100 100">
    <use xlink:href="rex.svg#ikon" />
</svg>
